What is emissivity and absorptivity?

About emissivity

A gray gas's total emissivity is defined as the ratio between the total emitted radiation over a path \(L\) (ignoring the self-absorption) and the maximum possible emission, aka. the black body emission, as shown below,

$$ \epsilon_t=\frac{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_g)\epsilon_\eta\mathrm{d}\eta}{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_g)\mathrm{d}\eta}=\frac{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_g)(1-\mathrm{e}^{-\kappa_\eta L})\mathrm{d}\eta}{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_g)\mathrm{d}\eta}=\frac{\pi\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_g)(1-\mathrm{e}^{-\kappa_\eta L})\mathrm{d}\eta}{\sigma T_g^4}, $$

where \(T_g\) is the temperature of the gas, \(I_{b\eta}\) is the black body emission intensity, \(\kappa_\eta\) is the spectral absorption coefficient, \(\eta\) is the wavenumber, and \(\sigma=5.67\times 10^{-8}\ \mathrm{W/m^2/K^4}\) is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant.

About absorptivity

A gray gas's total absorptivity is defined as the ratio between the total absorbed radiation over a path \(L\) and the emission from a black body surface, as shown below,

$$ \alpha_t=\frac{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_s)\epsilon_\eta\mathrm{d}\eta}{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_s)\mathrm{d}\eta}=\frac{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_s)(1-\mathrm{e}^{-\kappa_\eta L})\mathrm{d}\eta}{\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_s)\mathrm{d}\eta}=\frac{\pi\int_0^\infty I_{b\eta}(T_s)(1-\mathrm{e}^{-\kappa_\eta L})\mathrm{d}\eta}{\sigma T_s^4}, $$

where \(T_s\) is the temperature of the surface. \(\kappa_\eta\) in both equations can be obtained by the summation of the mixture's species' linear absorption coefficient \(\kappa_{\eta i}\).
